Is it possible to be at odds with yourself and still benefit from the struggle?

My husband jokes that the left and right hemispheres of my brain are in a power struggle. For that, I have my parents to thank.

When a kid grows up surrounded by her dad's 1970s textbooks and towering boxes of her mom's craft supplies, she’s bound to end up with some competing natures.

For my ninth birthday, my favorite gift was a Smithsonian Grow-Your-Own Crystals kit (yes, that's a thing). On one hand, I was super excited about the fact that I would be in possession of my very own petri dish and beaker. At the same time, there was nothing more beautiful to me than a sparkly purple rock. Especially one I grew myself.

Over time, this duality of interests began to present a problem. I was told I needed to choose: let my creativity run unbridled as a “real” artist, or focus my analytical nature into a hard skill.

I never chose.

Today, while my path has had a number of detours, I am thankful for that ongoing tug-of-war and for the unique perspective it gives me in every direction I take. As a portrait photographer, I bring empathy to the table, which puts my clients at ease in front of the camera. Alongside my husband, I get to give my toddler the same varied experiences my parents gave me.

Now, as a UX Designer and Front-End Developer, I bring an inherited desire to hunt down solutions and an ardent need to create and craft lovely things.

Discovering a profession that allows my to work for me is a gift, and it's one I would love to share.
