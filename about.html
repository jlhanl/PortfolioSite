A LITTLE BIT
ABOUT ME

Is is possible to be at odds with yourself and still benefit from that struggle?

At my ninth birthday party, my favorite gift was a Smithsonian Grow-Your-Own Crystals kit. One one hand, my inner chemist was super excited about the fact that I would be in possession of my very own beaker and petri dish.

On the other hand, to my inner pre-teen artist, there really wasn't anything more beautiful than the glittering edges of a purple gem. This truly was the best of both worlds. Not only could I study and observe the chemical reaction that built the crystal, I would actually grow the pretty rock myself!

This duality of interests went far beyond birthday gifts, and over time, it presented two competing forces: artist vs. scientist, right-brain vs. left-brain. As I moved onto college and into a career, there was pressure to choose: let creativity run unbridled as a true artist, or focus my analytical nature into a hard skill.

I chose both.

Now, I'm thankful for that ongoing tug-of-war and for the unique insights it gives me into broader perspectives. As a designer and photographer, I bring a natural tendency for empathy, and a passionate need to create and craft lovely experiences. As a researcher and web developer, my innate need to hunt down solutions provides a relentless motivation.

Discovering a profession that encompasses all of these things is a true gift, and it's one I'd love to share.
